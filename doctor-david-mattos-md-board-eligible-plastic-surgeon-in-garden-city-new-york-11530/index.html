How long after liposuction are pain and swelling normal?
most swelling resolves in first few weeks
Sep 22, 2020
After liposuction, 90% or so of the swelling resolves in the first month after surgery, with most of the dramatic improvement in the first two weeks. Patients are sore but are up and walking the day of surgery and may or may not require a few doses of narcotics, but not many at all. You may have some residual pain for the first week or two but most patients with normal jobs are able to go back to work within a week.
-Dr. David Mattos, Harvard-trained plastic surgeon

Liposuction swelling – how long until it goes down?
most in 3 months, final resolution by 1 year
Sep 22, 2020
The vast majority of the swelling goes down and resolves in the first 2-3 months. I typically recommend my patients to wear compression garments during this time. It helps the skin contract down. However, probably another 10% of the swelling can take up to a year to go away, and at this point is when you can judge the final outcome of the surgery.
Does touching your nose make it bigger over time?
it does not
Sep 20, 2020
Do not worry. You can touch your nose all you want and not make it any bigger over time. The only things that can change the size of your nose is growth during development, trauma, tumors, or surgically created changes. Outside of that, you can breathe easily. It won’t change the size of your nose.
-Dr. David Mattos, Harvard-trained plastic surgeon
