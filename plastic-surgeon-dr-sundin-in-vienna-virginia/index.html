Is under 1 week too early to return to working from home after surgery?
Maybe
Sep 16, 2020
Generally I recommend 2 weeks off work for a tummy tuck. The second week patient can often have small daily outings or a meeting for an hour or two. It really just depends on how intensive your course load will be following spring break. You wouldn't want to travel more than an 2 hours away from my surgeon during this period, and you wouldn't want to have any exams or major assignments.
What would you recommend for me to get the best results/flat belly back?
Depends how flat you would like it
Sep 8, 2020
I think you could have a nice result with liposuction and Renuvion/Jplasma skin tightening. There may be a small amount of excess skin at the bottom fold, this could be addressed with a mini tummy tuck either at the time of the initial liposuction or latter. The only downside is this would no allow for plication of the the upper abdominal muscles; to do this you would need a full tummy tuck.
Would I be a candidate for a mini tuck or would I be better for a full? Would I possibly need lipo?
Full tummy tuck
Aug 26, 2020
You would be best served with a full tummy tuck, as you have laxity above your belly button. You could add liposuction with renuvion skin tightening to your flanks and lower back to really bring in your waist.
Had a tummy tuck 34 years ago, how invasive would a scar revision be?
Minimal recovery
Aug 4, 2020
A scar revision or basically a mini tummy tuck with lipo would be an excellent option for you. Normal recovery 5-7days. It is a lot easier off a recovery than a full tummy tuck as there is no muscle diastasis Hence less pain, And shorter lifting restrictions.
Nanograft PRP for hair loss, is this safer and better than PRP +acell treatment?
Probably referring to nanofat
Aug 4, 2020
Different practices name similar procedures trademarked names. Nanofat, which is stem cells taken from your fat, combined with prp is possibly more effective for loss than prp and Acell. It is more time consuming and more of a procedures, so more $$$. One popular trademark procedure is the almi procedure. Both nanofat and exosomes may be the future of non invasive hair restoration. But neither is approved by the fda, so must be used off label.

You do not need a full vertical incision
Nov 19, 2019
You do not need a full vertical incision.   My only thoughts on why a plastic surgeon would have mentioned this is that you do have a high umbilical and aren't that loose.  It may be necessary to close the umbilicus defect with a extremely short (1/2 inch) vertical scar to allow the horizontal scar to be low and avoid too much tension on the incision.   This would most likely still be hidden in a bikini.   But a true vertical fleur de Lis scar would be inappropriate.
