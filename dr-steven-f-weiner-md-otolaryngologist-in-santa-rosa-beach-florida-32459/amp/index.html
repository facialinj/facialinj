What would be the best way to treat these acne scars?
Treating icepick and boxcar acne scars. Steven F. Weiner, MD
Sep 29, 2020
I would treat these repeatedly with TCA Cross. I do this every 2 weeks for 4-6 times. Consider adding RFM as well. These are not most likely tethered, so subcision is probably not required. Also consider ablative laser if not improving to degree you desire.
What type of acne scars do I have? What treatment would be the best option?
Icepick and boxcar scars in temple and cheek, Steven F. Weiner, MD
Sep 29, 2020
I would treat these scars using TCA Cross, 4-6 treatments are usually needed. I prefer to use RFM for treating acne scars due to its safety in all skin types and minimal downtime. RFM is done every 4-6 weeks. I don't think subcision is needed but would determine if there were tethering at time of exam and decide at that time.
What should I do now with my acne scarring after loads of treatments?
Boxcar and icepick scarring - failed several other procedures, darker skin type
Sep 23, 2020
I would treat with TCA Cross, 4-6 times at 2-4 week intervals. Consider ablative laser such as erbium if you still don't get the results you want. I don't think that dermabrasion will give you a result your are happy with because of PIH, further scarring as possible risks. One last consideration is serial punch excision.

Skin transplant for PIH?

Boxcar and icepick scarring - failed several other procedures

What is the best treatment for both boxcars and rolling scars?
Treating temple boxcar and rolling scars with RFM?
Sep 22, 2020
In general, RFM works well for rolling and boxcar scars and less for icepick scars. Depending on the scars, they might require subcision prior to RFM. I greatly prefer the Infini over the Viva, and prefer the Genius over both. TCA Cross is sometimes useful for the boxcar scars if they are small.

Is it more effective to do a 3 of Infini RF + Pico laser or 3 Fraxel 1550 + 1927?
How would you treat my asian skin acne scars?
Sep 15, 2020
I would treat your scars with TCA Cross for small boxcar and icepick scars. Subcision is needed for the atrophic rolling scars. I would then use Infini or Genius for the area of scarring. I haven't seen much improvements using the Fraxel Dual laser. The Pico laser also is not showing as good of results as hoped for. Ablative lasers are the only proven lasers for acne scars but I prefer RFM in your skin type. RFM has less chance of PIH, less downtime, and less risk then lasers.

Sep 12, 2020
Coolsculpting won't help visceral fat at all. I can help the subcutaneous fat only. There was one report of Emsculpt helping visceral fat but I have my doubts. Visceral fat can be treated with diet and exercise.
